The Proposal

Purpose: Provide a replicable framework for establishing a non-charitable Educational Trust Association (ETA) to preserve land, enable community learning, and ensure continuity of trust mission, values, and purpose across generations.

Trust Governance

Governance via a Council of Stewards per the Trust/Association Charter. Revenue from memberships, community learning, and events. Surpluses are carried forward for development, educational support, maintenance, equipment, and programs.

Trust-Held Assets

Land, infrastructure, and curriculum are developed and held collectively by the trustâ€”ensuring long-term stewardship.

Education-First

Activities (nursery development, workshops, renewable systems, internships, residencies) serve as curriculum to disseminate learning within and beyond the community.

How It Works

1) Form the Trust

Create a private trust to identify botanical stock and hold all assets (land, buildings, systems) in perpetuity for educational use.

2) Establish the Association

Adopt bylaws via a trust/membership charter. Trustees and members may have scaled access, governance, and voting rights within the operational framework.

3) Operate & Reinvest

Run programs and generate revenue via education, products, activities, and memberships. Carry forward surpluses; under current Canadian tax law, tax applies only to annual interest earned.

Note: Jurisdictional details vary. Plan in advance to consult local laws where you operate.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this a charity?

No. This is an educational association with assets held in a private trust. Surpluses are carried forward for programs and infrastructure maintenance; only interest on liquid capital carried forward is taxableâ€”similar to sports or condo associations.

How do members participate?

Through Council of Stewards appointments, annual or lifetime memberships, voting privileges, volunteerism, workshops, and seasonal events. There is no equity ownership.

Can other farms copy this?

Yes. The guides and templates are designed to be adapted in Canada and in jurisdictions with similar trust/association frameworks.

What about tax?

Operations are funded by memberships, educational activities, and products, with surpluses rolling forward. Only interest derived from carried-forward liquid capital is taxable.
