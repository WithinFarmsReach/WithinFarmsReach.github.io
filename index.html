Start Your Trust

A Member-Run Educational Trust Model
for Family Farms & Learning Estates

This site outlines a practical, lawful pathway to protect land and create transgenerational education-first stewardship—using Within Farm’s Reach as an example you can adapt.

Read the Guide Get Templates

The Proposal

Purpose: Provide a replicable framework for establishing a non-charitable Educational Trust Association (ETA) to preserve land, enable community learning, and ensure continuity of trust mission, values, and purpose across generations.

Member-Run

Revenue is generated from scaled memberships and community learning and other online and onsite events. Surpluses are carried forward for development, educational support, property maintenance, equipment, and programs. Programs show members who need support how to create and disseminate a sustainable model on their own land.

Trust-Held Assets

Land, infrastructure, and curriculum are developed and held collectively by the trust—ensuring long-term stewardship.

Education-First

Educational activities (development of horticultural nurseries, marketing, workshops, exemplar renewable systems, internship, residencies, etc.) represent the curriculum contents for dissemination of learning both within and beyond community.

How It Works

1) Form the Trust

Create a private trust to identify (botanical stock) and hold all assets (land, buildings, systems) in perpetuity for educational use.

2) Establish the Association

Adopt bylaws via trustees and a member charter. Depending on design trustees and /or members have access and voting rights within the Trust/Association Framework.

3) Operate & Reinvest

Run programs and generate revenue via educational opportunities, activities, and memberships. Carry forward surpluses; and according to current Canadian tax law, pay tax only on annual interest earned.

Note: Jurisdictional details vary. Plan in advance to consult local laws where you plan operate.

Case Study: Within Farm’s Reach

Our 6.2-acre historical property in Southwestern Ontario serves as the design example where this model can exist. The property was first developed beginning in the 1920’s by the second owner who worked in the botanical department at the local university. The result was many exotic species were established including heritage apples, walnuts, ubiquitous ferns, black raspberries, climbing tree hydrangeas, iris, forsythia, firebush and more recently tree species including cedar, pine, chestnut, and ginkgo. Two novel energy recovery systems (geothermal and heat-exhaust recovery) were recently designed and installed reducing energy costs by more than thirty percent. The main goal was to develop the property into a living ecological and community education campus that could operate across generations. Yet to succeed in such an ambitious endeavor, one will need the right kind of people. In my case, over the last 23 years, I developed two successful educational trust/associations. One was transient, bringing a biannual international conference to Canada in 2026 for the first time in 52 years. The second still exists and serves as a platform for community physician education. Of note details about both of these trust associations will be made available for those who elect to contact for the fine details of the model. This particular “Within Farm’s Reach” model was inspired by the highly recommended film The Common Ground (commongroundfilm.org) and aligns with the timely solutions to global warming that are presented. Any who contact Within Farm’s Reach is encouraged to first familiarize themselves with the mission and vision of The Common Ground film.

  • • 170-year-old home. Two streams run through
  • • Geothermal + heat-exhaust winter heat energy recovery systems
  • • Climbing tree hydrangeas & apple tree propagation & heritage apple wine production
  • • Walnut trees providing tinctures & pickled walnuts
  • • Ferns for Medicinals, florists, propagation & Ginkgo leaves for tincture and teas
  • • Seasonal education & small events

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this a charity?

No. This is an educational association with assets held in a private trust. Surpluses are carried forward for programs and infrastructure maintenance; only interest on liquid capital caried forward is taxable similar as a sports or condo association.

How do members participate?

Through board appointment, annual or lifetime memberships, voting privileges, volunteerism, participation in workshops, and seasonal events. There is no equity ownership.

Can other farms copy this?

Yes. The guides and templates are designed to be adapted in Canada and in jurisdictions with similar trust/association frameworks.

What about tax?

Operations are funded by memberships and surpluses roll forward as in educational, condo, or sports associations. Only interest derived from carried-forward liquid capital is taxed.
